The concept we delivered for them essentially makes the museum and its collection more accessible.

Brief

Moderna asked us to "Make a proposal for a smartphone application with emphasis on content in terms of knowledge of and proximity to the works of art".

Background

With the arrival of a new major photograhy museum in Stockholm, Moderna Museet were going through a period of reflection regarding their position at the forefront of art & culture in Sweden.

One key insight was that Moderna Museet has one of the largest collections of art in Europe, but many pieces are rarely shown, due to space limitations.

Solution

Features of the smartphone app:

An integrated map system helps with navigation to and around the museum.

Visits can be planned in detail, based on interests, duration of visit & the people you are with

Suggested routes around the museum can be created, allowing a more personal and rewarding experience

The story of a different piece of art is told every day, spreading knowledge & making art more accessible

Browse parts of the collection that are not currently on show

Visitors can decide how much they want to know about each piece

Visitors can save their favourite pieces of art, helping them to remember details, and plan future visits

QR codes link visitors to a guide that takes the form of a puppet version of Robert Rauschenberg's Monogram (a very important piece for the museum). The puppet introduces art in an entertaining and informative way
