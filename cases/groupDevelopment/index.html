I believe in teamwork, and I have experience in facilitating group development.

Facilitating the Hyper Island Way

Over the last two years I’ve worked as a Facilitator for Hyper Island during its introductory week for new students.

Hyper Island’s methodology is different to almost every other learning establishment in the world. The introductory week is an extremely intense, joyful and rewarding experience, where participants learn not only about themselves, but a whole range of other things.

The focus is on learning by doing, group dynamics, feedback, reflection, and above all, creating a constructive social arena. The importance of “How” a task is completed is given equal importance to “What” is actually done.

I’m comfortable teaching the methodology to large and small groups.

Background

Hyper Island’s methodology has it’s roots in the UGL system developed by the Swedish Military, and the research of Susan Wheelan. A good place to start learning about group development would be Susan Wheelan's book, Creating Effective Teams.

Application

I use Hyper Island's Methodology in my working practices, and I am comfortable either leading the process or being an active member of a group.
