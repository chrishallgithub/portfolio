RFID Magic Music Player
Overview

I love music. I love the portability of Mp3. I hate the interaction of most Mp3 players.

I built a web-based Mp3 player that is controlled by either RFID input, or user input on a web page. I used Arduino, HTML5, Javascript, jQuery and a bunch of PHP. If you forget the technical side though, it seems to work by magic.

Background

I had a party at my apartment. I found myself spending way too much time organising the music than actually talking to my friends. When I thought about this, I found myself yearning for the good olâ€™ days of CDs or even LPs. Back then, interaction was minimal; choose a song to play, find the CD/ LP, and erm, play it. The process also involved admiring the artwork, and would even spark a conversation about which song to play. It seemed a much more human way to interact with technology.

I realised a few things; most importantly that poor interaction can make you dislike something you love.

Aim

I wanted something more responsive; something more social. I wanted to use my tech knowledge to access my music collection in a way that required minimal interaction, would be fun, allow me to rediscover forgot artwork, and even start a conversation or two.
