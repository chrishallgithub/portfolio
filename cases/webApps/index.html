Webapps
Overview

Webapps are just like smartphone apps, but work on anything with a web browser (including smart phones). I love the openness of this, and so I made a few simple webapps to play around with the idea. Click on the images above to try them out.

Where’s Hyper?

Where’s Hyper is a webapp that pinpoints your location and then uses Googlemaps API to display the most direct driving route to Hyper Island on a map.

This app requires your geolocation, and so to see it in action, please grant the request for you location.

I also converted the webapp into an Android app, using Eclipse and Phonegap.

The Android app also sends a proximity alert to the user when they are near Hyper Island, inviting them to drop by and say hello.
20

When working on a computer it is recommended to take a break every 20 minutes, and focus on something 20 feet away, for 20 seconds.

20 is a webapp that plays a sound and/or displays a pop-up alert every 20 minutes to remind us that it is time to look after our eyes. The page just needs to be left open in a tab on a browser for it to work.
